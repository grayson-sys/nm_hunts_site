# Wyoming Big Game Draw System -- Deer & Elk

**Last Updated:** March 2026
**Applies To:** 2025-2026 and 2026-2027 hunting seasons

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### System Type: Hybrid Preference Point / Random

Wyoming uses a **hybrid system** combining preference points and random draw. This is NOT a pure preference point system and NOT a pure bonus point system.

- **75% of available licenses** in each hunt area and license type are allocated in the **preference point drawing**, where applicants are ranked by accumulated preference points (highest first).
- **25% of available licenses** are allocated in a **random drawing**, open to all applicants regardless of point totals.

This 75/25 split applies within each hunt area and license type separately.

### How Preference Points Rank Applicants

- Allocation starts with applicants holding the highest number of preference points and works downward until all preference-point licenses are filled.
- For nonresident full-price elk, deer, and antelope, preference points are **only considered for the applicant's first choice** in the preference point drawing.
- If you draw your first-choice license in the preference point drawing, **all accumulated preference points for that species are forfeited**.
- Points are species-specific, not hunt-area specific.

### Separate Draws by Weapon Type?

Wyoming does NOT run entirely separate draws by weapon type. Instead, weapon type is embedded in the **license type** system:

| License Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Type 1** | Any legal weapon; most common full-price tag. Holders can also purchase archery stamp to hunt September archery season. |
| **Type 2** | Full-price; different season dates, area boundaries, or antler restrictions than Type 1. |
| **Type 6** | Reduced-price antlerless tag (cow/calf or doe/fawn). Can be held in addition to a primary tag. |
| **Type 9** | Archery-only license (full price). No separate archery stamp needed. |

Each license type within each hunt area is drawn separately. Applicants choose a specific license type + hunt area combination.

### Number of Choices Per Application

Applicants may submit **three (3) choices** for their preferred hunt area/license type combinations. The draw processes all first choices before moving to second choices, then third choices.

### What Happens If You Don't Draw Any Choices?

- Your license fee is refunded (application fee is NOT refunded).
- You do NOT automatically receive a preference point. You must separately purchase a preference point during the preference point-only application period (July 1 -- November 2) if you want to accumulate a point for that year.
- You may pursue tags through:
  - The **leftover drawing** (a random draw with residents and nonresidents in the same pool; no additional application fee).
  - **Over-the-counter** purchase of any remaining tags after the leftover draw.

### Special Management Permits

Type 6 (antlerless) licenses serve as the primary special management tool. These are reduced-price tags used to address specific herd-size management concerns and can be held in addition to a primary (Type 1/2/9) tag.

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### Can Hunters Turn In a Tag to Keep/Gain Points?

**No.** Once a license is issued, it becomes the permanent property of the licensee. Licenses shall not be canceled or fees refunded after issuance, except in limited hardship circumstances:

- Death of the hunter or immediate family member
- Illness or injury preventing hunting
- Jury duty or military service
- Forest fire or other force majeure preventing hunting opportunity
- WGFD administrative error

A License Review Board can consider other hardship cases (requires 2 of 3 board members to approve).

### Effect on Points if Refund Is Granted

If a hardship refund is granted, **all accumulated preference points** (including any point earned that year) are restored. However, application fees and preference point fees are not refunded.

### Application Withdrawal Before Draw

You can voluntarily withdraw an application before the drawing for a $5 processing fee. Withdrawal must be initiated through the ELS (Electronic Licensing System) by the published deadline.

### Can You Buy a Point Without Applying?

**Yes** -- but only for nonresidents (for elk, deer, and antelope). Purchase a preference point during the **preference point-only application period: July 1 -- November 2**. There is no application fee when purchasing a point only.

For residents, preference points for elk, deer, and antelope are NOT applicable (residents have access to general/OTC licenses). Residents CAN accumulate preference points for moose and bighorn sheep.

### Losing Points

You lose ALL accumulated preference points for a species if you **fail to either apply or purchase a preference point for two (2) consecutive years**.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

As of the 2026 draw cycle, the maximum number of nonresident elk preference points is approximately **20 points**. Many top elk units now require maximum points, and even at max points the draw has effectively become random due to the large pool of max-point holders ("point creep").

Specific maximums for deer: NOT FOUND - needs manual verification (likely 18-20+ based on the system's history since the early 2000s).

## 3. RESIDENT VS. NONRESIDENT RULES

### Tag Allocation Percentages

**Deer and Antelope (Limited Quota):**
- **80% of tags** go to residents
- **20% of tags** go to nonresidents

**Elk (Limited Quota):**
- **84% of tags** go to residents (Commission-approved)
- **16% of tags** go to nonresidents

### Separate Draws

**Yes.** Residents and nonresidents are drawn in **separate pools** during the initial draw.

Exception: The **leftover drawing** places residents and nonresidents in a **single combined pool** (random draw, no preference points).

### Nonresident Special vs. Regular License Split

Within the nonresident allocation, tags are further split:

- **60%** to the **regular-price** nonresident draw
- **40%** to the **special (full-price)** nonresident draw

By paying a significantly higher license fee, applicants enter the special draw with a separate, smaller applicant pool -- generally better odds.

### Nonresident Caps

Caps are applied **per unit/hunt area**, not statewide. The percentage allocation (16% elk, 20% deer/antelope) applies to each individual hunt area's quota.

### Waiting Periods

- No general waiting period after drawing an elk or deer tag.
- **Moose and bighorn sheep:** Successful hunters must wait **5 years** before re-applying.

### Residency Requirements

- Must maintain a fixed, permanent domicile in Wyoming for **no less than one (1) full year** immediately preceding the date of application.
- Must physically reside in Wyoming for at least **180 days per calendar year** to maintain residency.
- Cannot claim residency in any other state, territory, or country for any purpose.
- Property ownership or a Wyoming mailing address alone is NOT sufficient.
- **Military exception:** Active-duty military, spouses, and minor children qualify after 90 days stationed in Wyoming.

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### Resident General Elk -- OTC

- **Available OTC for residents only.** General elk licenses go on sale **July 16** (2026).
- **Unlimited** in the number issued statewide.
- Valid in any hunt area open to general license hunting, subject to that area's season dates and weapon restrictions.
- Price: **$57.00** (same as limited-quota Type 1 resident elk).
- Nonresidents **cannot** purchase general elk licenses OTC. All nonresident elk licenses require draw participation.

### Resident General Deer -- OTC

- **Available OTC for residents only.** General deer licenses go on sale **July 16** (2026).
- **Unlimited** in the number issued statewide.
- Valid in any hunt area open to general license hunting.
- Price: **$42.00** (same as limited-quota Type 1 resident deer).
- Maximum of one general deer license per person per year.
- Nonresidents **cannot** purchase general deer licenses OTC.

### OTC vs. Draw -- How to Tell

- **General hunt areas** (OTC): Available to residents without draw; typically have more liberal seasons and higher hunter density.
- **Limited quota hunt areas** (Draw): Require application; controlled hunter numbers; often better quality hunting with extended seasons.
- The annual regulation booklet and WGFD Hunt Planner identify which areas are general vs. limited quota.

### Leftover Tags

After the initial draw and leftover drawing, remaining tags are sold **first-come, first-served** to both residents and nonresidents. These become available after the leftover draw results (typically early-mid July).

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

### Landowner Preference Program (LPP)

Wyoming has a landowner license program (not called "LPP" officially, but commonly referenced).

### Eligibility Requirements

- Must own at least **160 contiguous deeded acres** within the specific hunt area.
- Land must provide suitable habitat (food, cover, water) for the species.
- The species must use the property for at least **2,000 "animal use days"** over the previous year.
- Land purchased or subdivided primarily to obtain tags is ineligible (though enforcement is inconsistent per WGFD).
- Both resident AND nonresident landowners may qualify under identical rules.

### Tag Details

- Maximum of **two (2) licenses per species per parcel** annually.
- Tags are issued to the qualifying landowner or their **immediate family members**.
- Landowner licenses are priced the same as the corresponding standard license type.

### Transferability

**Tags are NOT transferable.** Wyoming landowner tags cannot be sold, given away, or transferred to non-family members.

- Multiple legislative attempts to make landowner tags transferable have **failed**. Senate File 51 and Senate File 118 both proposed transferability but were defeated.
- The WGFD received over 2,100 public comments opposing transferable landowner tags.
- This distinguishes Wyoming from states like Colorado and New Mexico, where landowner tags can be sold on the open market.

### Volume Relative to Public Draw Tags

Exact numbers of landowner tags issued vs. public draw tags are not published in a single consolidated source. However, the volume is relatively small compared to the total public draw allocation, as each qualifying parcel receives at most 2 tags per species.
