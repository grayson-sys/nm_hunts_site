# Idaho Big Game Draw System -- Deer and Elk

*Last updated: March 2026*
*Sources cited inline. All information subject to change; verify with IDFG before applying.*

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### Draw Type: Pure Random Lottery (No Points)

Idaho uses a **straight random lottery** for all controlled hunts. There is **no preference point or bonus point system**. Every applicant receives one random number, and tags are allocated from lowest number to highest until the quota is filled. First-time applicants and 20-year veterans have identical odds. ([HuntScore](https://huntscore.com/blog/idaho-draw-process), [onX Hunt](https://www.onxmaps.com/hunt/blog/hunting-application-details-idaho))

A group application receives a single random number; if drawn, all members are drawn together. A group containing even one nonresident is treated as a nonresident application. ([HuntScore](https://huntscore.com/blog/idaho-draw-process))

### Weapon-Specific Hunts

Idaho does **not** run separate draws by weapon type. Instead, individual controlled hunt numbers specify the weapon restriction (any-weapon, archery-only, muzzleloader-only, or short-range weapon). Hunters choose a specific 4-digit controlled hunt number that already encodes the weapon type, unit, species, and sex. Archery and muzzleloader hunters must also purchase the appropriate **permit** (separate from tags). ([IDFG Controlled Hunt Application](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/controlled/apply), [eRegulations](https://www.eregulations.com/idaho/hunting/hunting-seasons-and-dates))

### Number of Choices per Application

- **Controlled hunts (deer, elk, pronghorn, fall bear, turkey):** Applicants may select a **first choice** and an optional **second choice** controlled hunt number. ([IDFG](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/controlled/apply))
- **Nonresident general tag draw (new for 2026):** Applicants may list up to **5 hunt choices** per application. ([IDFG 2026 NR article](https://idfg.idaho.gov/article/everything-nonresident-hunter-needs-know-about-2026-tag-drawing))
- **Group size:** Up to 4 hunters for deer/elk/pronghorn; up to 2 for turkey, bear, moose, sheep, goat. ([IDFG](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/controlled/apply))

### What Happens If You Don't Draw

You simply do not receive a controlled hunt tag. There are no points accumulated, no consolation mechanisms, and no waitlist. You may still:
- Purchase a general season (OTC) tag for deer or elk (residents).
- Apply in the **second controlled hunt drawing** (August 5-15), which includes unfilled and unclaimed tags from the first draw. Results posted ~August 25; remaining leftovers sold first-come, first-served at 10 a.m. MDT that same day. ([IDFG](https://idfg.idaho.gov/article/second-big-game-controlled-hunt-drawing-application-period-aug-5-15-0))
- Purchase leftover controlled hunt tags sold first-come, first-served starting late August.

### General vs. Controlled Hunt Tags

- **General season tags**: Available over-the-counter (OTC) to residents; available to nonresidents via draw starting 2026 (previously first-come, first-served). Valid in general season units/zones that are not restricted to controlled-hunt-only.
- **Controlled hunt tags**: Required for specific units with limited entry. Issued only through the controlled hunt draw (or second draw / leftover sales). ([IDFG](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/tag/controlled))

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### No Point System

Idaho has **no preference points, bonus points, or loyalty points** of any kind. There is nothing to bank, lose, or purchase. Every year is a fresh start. ([onX Hunt](https://www.onxmaps.com/hunt/blog/hunting-application-details-idaho), [HuntScore](https://huntscore.com/blog/idaho-draw-process))

### Can You Buy a Point Without Applying?

No. There are no points to buy.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

N/A -- no point system exists.

### Tag Turn-Back / Refund Rules

Hunters who draw a controlled hunt tag or purchase a general season tag may return it under the following refund schedule:

| Postmarked By     | Refund % (Tag Fee Only) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Before April 1    | 75%                    |
| Before June 30    | 50%                    |
| Before August 31  | 25%                    |
| After September 1 | No refund              |

- **License fees and application fees are NOT refundable** once used.
- For **moose, bighorn sheep, or mountain goat**, only tag fees are refunded; license and application fees are forfeited regardless.
- Controlled hunt tags can be returned to the department; returned tags become available through the second draw or returned tag sales.
- Returning a tag has **no effect on future draw odds** (since there are no points). ([IDFG Returned Tags](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/tag/returns), [IDFG NR Refund](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/forms/nonresident-refund))

### Returned Tag Sales (2026 Dates)

Returned general and controlled hunt tags are resold on scheduled dates:
- April 23
- June 25
- August 6 (second tags for residents)
- September 17

Sales begin at 10:00 a.m. MT via GoOutdoorsIdaho.com, regional offices, license vendors, or phone (1-800-554-8685). ([IDFG](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/tag/returns))

## 3. RESIDENT VS. NONRESIDENT RULES

### Residency Requirements

A person must have been **domiciled in Idaho for at least 6 continuous months** immediately preceding the date of application with bona fide intent to make Idaho their permanent home. Proof required: two documents bearing name and address (utility bills, mortgage statements, voter registration, etc.) dated at least 6 months prior. ([IDFG Residency](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/residency-requirements))

**Military exception:** Active-duty military stationed in Idaho may purchase resident licenses after 30 continuous days of residency via signed affidavit (includes spouse and children under 18 in same household).

### Tag Allocation -- Controlled Hunts

- Nonresidents are limited to **no more than 10% of available controlled hunt tags** per species.
- If a controlled hunt has **10 or fewer total permits**, a maximum of **1 tag** may go to a nonresident.
- Resident and nonresident applicants are drawn from **separate pools** within the same draw. ([HuntScore](https://huntscore.com/blog/idaho-draw-process), [onX Hunt](https://www.onxmaps.com/hunt/blog/hunting-application-details-idaho))

### Tag Allocation -- General Season (Nonresident Caps)

Starting 2021, nonresident general season deer and elk tags are capped at **10-15% of total hunters** per elk zone or deer unit, based on the 5-year average of hunter participation. Starting **2026**, these nonresident general season tags are issued through a **mandatory draw** (replacing the previous first-come, first-served system). ([IDFG](https://idfg.idaho.gov/press/fg-commission-approves-changes-nonresident-participation-general-season-deer-and-elk-hunts))

### Waiting Periods

No formal waiting periods for nonresidents. However, demand far exceeds supply for many units, and the draw is annual with no accumulated advantage.

### Nonresident Caps

Caps are applied **per unit (deer) and per zone (elk)**, not statewide. Each unit/zone has its own nonresident tag quota. ([IDFG NR Quotas](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/tag/quotas/nonresident))

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### Residents

| Species | OTC Availability | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| **Deer** | Yes -- unlimited | General season tags available for any GMU with a general season. Purchase after controlled hunt results (~late June/early July). |
| **Elk** | Yes -- unlimited in uncapped zones; limited in capped zones | Most elk zones are uncapped (unlimited resident tags). Several zones are **capped** with a finite OTC quota sold first-come, first-served. |

**Capped elk zones** (examples from 2025 season; list changes annually): Bear River B, Big Desert B, Diamond Creek A & B, Dworshak A & B, Elk City A & B, Lolo A & B, Middle Fork A & B, Pioneer B, Salmon B, Sawtooth A & B, Selway A & B, Smoky-Bennett B, South Hills B. ([IDFG Resident Capped Elk](https://idfg.idaho.gov/licenses/tag/resident/elk))

**Resident OTC tag sale timing:** Capped elk zone tags go on sale with designated sale dates (check IDFG annually). Uncapped elk zone tags and deer tags available after controlled hunt draw results are posted.

**Important rule:** Residents who apply for a controlled elk hunt must **wait 5 days** after capped elk zone tag sales open before purchasing a capped zone tag.

### Nonresidents

Starting **2026**, nonresident general season deer and elk tags are **no longer OTC**. They are issued exclusively through a draw (application Dec. 5-15, 2025 for the 2026 season). Previously (~2021-2025), nonresident tags were sold first-come, first-served with unit/zone quotas capped at 10-15% of average hunter participation. ([IDFG](https://idfg.idaho.gov/article/everything-nonresident-hunter-needs-know-about-2026-tag-drawing))

**Nonresident OTC species (still available):** Bear, mountain lion, turkey, wolf.

### OTC Tag Prices

Same as the tag prices listed in Section 4 above. No premium for OTC vs. drawn tags.

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

### Landowner Appreciation Program (LAP)

Idaho operates the **Landowner Appreciation Program (LAP)**, which allocates a portion of controlled hunt tags to qualifying landowners in controlled hunt areas. ([IDFG LAP](https://idfg.idaho.gov/sites/default/files/seasons-rules-big-game-2025-lap.pdf), [IDAPA 13.01.04.400](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/idaho/IDAPA-13.01.04.400))

### Eligibility

- Property must provide **significant habitat** for deer, elk, pronghorn, or black bear.
- Property must be **registered** with IDFG (submit deed, tax assessment, property map to regional office).
- Property must be located within a **controlled hunt area** (not general season-only units).

### Acreage Requirements and Tag Allocation

| Acreage             | Tags Available                    |
|---------------------|-----------------------------------|
| 320 - 639 acres     | Eligible for leftover LAP tags only (max 1 per species) |
| 640 - 4,999 acres   | 1 tag per species via LAP draw    |
| 5,000+ acres        | Up to 2 tags per species via LAP draw |

### Volume Relative to Public Draw

- LAP tags are set at **up to 10% additional** controlled hunt tags on top of the public allocation.
- In subsequent years, up to **25%** of controlled hunt tags may be issued as LAP tags, but **only if** the hunt is over-subscribed by eligible LAP applicants.

### Tags Tied to Property vs. Unit-Wide

Tags are tied to the **controlled hunt area** in which the registered property is located, not to the specific property parcel. The tag is valid unit-wide for hunting (not confined to the landowner's property).

### Transferability

- Successful LAP applicants **may designate another eligible individual** to receive the tag.
- **It is unlawful to sell or market LAP controlled hunt tags.** Violation results in 3-year ineligibility for the program.

### Application Timeline

- Applications for 640+ acre properties: **May 15 - June 15** (entered in random LAP draw).
- Leftover LAP tags (320+ acres): Available starting **July 15** on a first-come, first-served basis.
- Applicants with 640+ acres receive **one entry per 640 acres** held, providing larger landowners with proportionally better odds.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR APPLICANTS

1. **No points system** -- Idaho is a pure random lottery. Apply every year; your odds never improve from prior applications.
2. **2026 is a major change year for nonresidents** -- General season deer and elk tags now require a draw (Dec 5-15 application). The old first-come-first-served system is gone.
3. **Nonresidents are capped at ~10-15%** of tags per unit (deer) or zone (elk) for both controlled and general season hunts.
4. **Residents have unlimited OTC deer tags** and unlimited elk tags in uncapped zones. Several elk zones are capped with limited OTC quotas.
5. **Second draw opportunity** in August for unfilled/unclaimed controlled hunt tags.
6. **Draw odds data is highly accessible** -- API + CSV/JSON/Excel exports available from 1998 onward.
