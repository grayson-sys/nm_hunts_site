*Last updated: March 2026*
*Primary sources: ODFW (myodfw.com), eRegulations, Huntin' Fool, onX Hunt*

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### System Type: Modified Preference Point System (Hybrid)

Oregon uses a **modified preference point system** for controlled (limited-entry) hunts. It is a hybrid: 75% of tags go to the highest preference point holders, and 25% are awarded by pure random draw among all first-choice applicants regardless of points.

### How It Works - Exact Math

For each individual controlled hunt code:

1. **Preference Point Drawing (75%):** 75% of available tags are awarded to first-choice applicants with the most accumulated preference points. If there are more applicants at the highest point level than tags available in this pool, a random draw occurs among those tied applicants.
2. **Random Drawing (25%):** The remaining 25% of tags are issued randomly among ALL first-choice applicants (including those who were unsuccessful in the preference draw).
3. **Exception - 3 or fewer tags:** For hunt codes with 3 or fewer tags, ALL tags are issued in the Preference Point drawing (no random pool).

**Applies to:** Deer (100 series), Elk (200 series), Pronghorn (400 series), Antlerless Deer (600 series), and Spring Bear (700 series).

**Does NOT apply to:** Premium hunts (L, M, N series), Bighorn Sheep (500 series), and Rocky Mountain Goat (900 series) -- these are **pure random draw with NO preference points**.

### Earning Points

- You receive **one preference point** each year you apply for a controlled hunt series and do NOT draw your first-choice hunt.
- If you draw your **second through fifth choice**, you still gain a preference point AND receive the tag.
- Points are tracked per hunt **series** (e.g., 100-series deer, 200-series elk), not per individual hunt code.
- Points do not expire and accumulate indefinitely.

### Losing Points

- If you draw your **first-choice** hunt, your preference points for that series reset to **zero**, whether or not you actually purchase the tag.
- **Exception:** Pioneer License holders and Resident Disabled Veteran License holders age 65+ have points reset to **one** (not zero) upon drawing first choice.

### Separate Draws by Weapon Type?

Oregon does **not** run completely separate draws by weapon type. However, controlled hunts are coded by weapon type (rifle, archery, muzzleloader) within the hunt series. Applicants choose specific hunt codes that correspond to weapon types. General season tags are also sold separately by weapon type (e.g., General Archery Western Oregon Tag vs. General Any Legal Weapon Western Oregon Tag).

### Number of Choices per Application

- **Up to 5 hunt choices** per application per hunt series.
- First choice is the only one that uses preference points. Choices 2-5 are considered only if tags remain after first-choice draws.
- You can submit **one application per hunt series** (e.g., one for the 100-series deer, one for the 200-series elk).

### What Happens If You Don't Draw Any Choices?

- You gain **one preference point** for that series.
- You can still purchase a **general season tag** (OTC) if available for your desired species/area.
- Leftover controlled hunt tags become available: Spring bear on March 15 at 10 a.m. PST; all other big game on July 1 at 10 a.m. PST (online only, first-come first-served).

### Party Applications

- Up to **18 hunters** may apply as a party for deer and elk.
- Up to **2 hunters** for antelope.
- **No party applications** for bighorn sheep or Rocky Mountain goat.
- Party member preference points are **averaged** (rounded up from 0.51, down from 0.50).

### General vs. Controlled Hunts

- **General season hunts:** Over-the-counter (OTC) tags available to anyone with a valid hunting license. No draw required. Primarily Western Oregon deer and elk.
- **Controlled hunts:** Limited-entry, require application and draw. All Eastern Oregon deer hunts (as of 2026), many elk hunts, and all pronghorn, sheep, goat, and premium hunts.

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### Point Saver Option (Buying a Point Without Applying for a Hunt)

Yes, Oregon allows hunters to apply for a **"Point Saver"** to gain a preference point without entering the draw for a specific hunt. Details:

- Enter the **Point Saver code** as your first choice for a given series.
- You **cannot** apply for other hunt choices within the same series if you use a Point Saver.
- Point Savers are available during:
  - **Main application period:** December 1 through May 15
  - **Points-only purchase period:** July 1 through November 30
- You can only earn **one preference point per year** per series.
- A valid **Annual Hunting License** is required to purchase points.
- The application fee ($10 in 2026) applies.

### Tag Turn-Back / Surrender

- Standard policy: If you draw your **first-choice** hunt, your preference points reset to zero **whether or not you purchase the tag**. There is no standard mechanism to turn back a drawn tag and retain points.
- After the application period closes, there are generally **no modifications or takebacks**.
- In rare exceptional circumstances (e.g., COVID-19 pandemic), ODFW has restored points to hunters who returned tags, but this is **not standard policy**.
- Unpurchased drawn tags may become leftover tags available to other hunters.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

Based on the 2026 draw cycle (Huntin' Fool reporting):

| Species | Maximum Preference Points |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Deer (100 series) | **33 points** |
| Elk (200 series) | **32 points** |
| Pronghorn (400 series) | **33 points** |

Point caps increase by one each year. The preference point system has been in place since approximately 1993.

## 3. RESIDENT VS. NONRESIDENT RULES

### Residency Requirements

- A **resident** must have physically resided in Oregon for **not less than 6 consecutive months** immediately prior to applying.
- Merely owning property or paying property taxes in Oregon does **not** qualify as residency.
- Claiming resident privileges in another state disqualifies Oregon residency.
- Transition rule: An Oregon resident who moves out-of-state before season ends may complete the current season as a resident if documents were purchased while still a resident.

### Nonresident Tag Allocation Caps

| Species | Max % to Nonresidents |
|---------|----------------------|
| Deer | **5%** of controlled hunt tags |
| Elk | **5%** of controlled hunt tags |
| Black Bear | **5%** of controlled hunt tags |
| Pronghorn | **3%** of controlled hunt tags |
| Bighorn Sheep | **5-10%** of tags |
| Rocky Mountain Goat | **5-10%** of tags |

- For hunts with **35 or fewer total tags**, a maximum of **1 tag** goes to nonresidents/outfitters.
- **2.5% of nonresident tags** are reserved for the **Guides and Outfitters Program** before the main draw.

### Combined or Separate Draws?

Residents and nonresidents participate in the **same draw** but with the percentage caps enforced. Nonresidents do not receive any preference advantage beyond their accumulated preference points.

### Waiting Periods

- **No waiting periods** for deer, elk, or antelope (you can apply every year).
- **Bighorn sheep and Rocky Mountain goat:** One tag per **lifetime** maximum.

### Nonresident General Season Tags

Nonresidents **can** purchase general season (OTC) tags for Western Oregon deer and elk without entering a draw, subject to availability. There is no quota on general season OTC tags for nonresidents -- they are unlimited.

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### OTC Deer Tags - YES (Western Oregon Only)

Western Oregon deer tags are available over-the-counter with **no quota** (unlimited availability):

| Tag Type | Season Dates (2026) | Purchase Deadline |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| General Any Legal Weapon - Western Oregon | Oct. 3 - Nov. 6 | Oct. 2 |
| General Archery - Western Oregon | Aug. 29 - Sept. 27 | Aug. 28 |

**Eastern Oregon deer:** As of 2026, **ALL** Eastern Oregon deer hunts are controlled (draw-only). Eastern Oregon was restructured from Wildlife Management Units (WMUs) to Deer Hunt Areas based on GPS collar data.

### OTC Elk Tags - YES (Select Zones)

Several elk general season tags are available OTC with **no quota**:

| Tag Type | Season Dates (2026) | Purchase Deadline |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| General West Cascade Elk (Any Legal Weapon) | Nov. 7 - 13 | Nov. 6 |
| General Eastern OR Rocky Mountain Elk (2nd Season) | Nov. 7 - 15 | Nov. 6 |
| General Coast Bull Elk (1st Season) | Nov. 14 - 17 | Nov. 13 |
| General Coast Bull Elk (2nd Season) | Nov. 21 - 27 | Nov. 20 |
| General Archery Elk (Western/Eastern) | Aug. 29 - Sept. 27 | Aug. 28 |

**Note:** Some eastern Oregon archery elk units have been converted to controlled-only for 2026 -- consult regulations pages 46-47.

### OTC Tag Prices

OTC general season tags are the same price as controlled hunt tags (see Section 4). The tag fee is: Resident deer $32, Resident elk $55, Nonresident deer $500, Nonresident elk $660 (2026 prices). No application fee for OTC tags.

### Other OTC Opportunities

- **Black Bear:** Spring (April 1 - May 31) and Fall (Aug. 1 - Dec. 31) -- many units are general season.
- **Cougar:** Year-round (Jan. 1 - Dec. 31).
- **Mountain Lion:** General season available.

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

### Program: Landowner Preference (LOP) Program

Established in 1981 by the Oregon Legislature to acknowledge the contribution of private lands to wildlife support and provide compensation to landowners.

### Eligibility

- Must be a **registered property owner** (one representative per property).
- Registration fee: **$35**.
- Must file tag distribution forms and submit tax lot maps before hunt deadlines.

### Acreage Requirements and Tag Allocations

| Contiguous Acreage | Number of Tags | Qualifying Hunts |
|--------------------|----------------|------------------|
| 40 - 159 acres | 2 | Antlerless deer/elk, Western Oregon hunts only |
| 160 - 400 acres | 2 | All hunts |
| 401 - 1,600 acres | 3 | All hunts |
| 1,601 - 2,500 acres | 4 | All hunts |
| 2,501 - 5,000 acres | 5 | All hunts |
| 5,001 - 10,000 acres | 6 | All hunts |
| 10,001 - 20,000 acres | 8 | All hunts |
| 20,001 - 40,000 acres | 10 | All hunts |
| 40,001 - 80,000 acres | 12 | All hunts |
| 160,000+ acres | 14 | All hunts |

### Tags Tied to Property vs. Unit-Wide?

Tags are **property-specific**. LOP tag holders "may only hunt on the property for which they are registered." Tags are NOT transferable between properties.

### Transferability

- **Half** of allocated tags (rounded up) for antlered game (one deer, one elk) may be issued to **non-family members**.
- **All** antlerless tags may be transferred to **immediate family members** or a designated non-family person.
- Pronghorn LOP tags: Available **only to landowners and family members** (not transferable to non-family).
- Tags are **not commercially saleable** on an open market -- they must be distributed through ODFW's system.

### Volume Relative to Public Draw Tags

- LOP tags come from the same controlled hunt tag pool but are set aside before the public draw.
- Specific buck deer hunts have caps of **5 tags or 10%** of total hunt allocation for LOP.
- In WMUs where mule deer populations are below the management objective, LOP tags are limited.
- The Commission adopted a **two-tier formula** (replacing the previous three-tier) for mule deer LOP tags, considering both population levels and growth rates.

### Recent Changes (2026)

- LOP tags are **not available** for Aug. 15 - Dec. 31 youth elk hunts or Melrose 223T elk hunts.
- Oregon's landowner hunting tag program was poised to become **permanent** legislation in 2025.
- New two-tier allocation formula for mule deer tags adopted by the Commission.
