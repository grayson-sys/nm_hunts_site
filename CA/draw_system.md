# California Big Game Draw System -- Deer and Elk

Last updated: March 2026
Research compiled from CDFW official sources, Huntin' Fool, goHUNT, onX Hunt, HuntScore, and eRegulations.

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### Overall System Type

California uses a **preference point system** for both deer (premium tags) and elk. This is NOT a bonus point system -- preference points function as "a place in line: first in, first out." Higher point holders are drawn before lower point holders in the preference round, and among applicants with equal points, winners are selected randomly.

### Deer Draw

- **Premium deer tags** (X zones and other limited-entry hunts) are allocated through the Big Game Drawing.
- **90% of tags** are awarded in the **Preference Point Draw** to applicants with the most preference points.
- **10% of tags** are awarded in the **Random Draw** to all applicants regardless of point level.
- **Apprentice (youth) hunts** for hunters 16 and under use a **50/50 split**: 50% preference point draw, 50% random draw.
- Applicants may select **up to 3 hunt choices** in preferential order. Everyone's first choice is considered before anyone's second or third choice.
- If you are not drawn for any of your 3 choices, you earn 1 preference point for deer.
- Points are **species-specific**, NOT hunt-specific, zone-specific, or weapon-specific. One pool of deer preference points covers all deer hunts.
- Party applications are allowed for deer with up to 6 people.
- Drawing a tag as your **first choice** resets your preference points to zero.
- If drawn on your second or third choice, you retain your points (they are not consumed).

### Elk Draw

- Elk tags are issued through the same Big Game Drawing but with different rules.
- Applicants select **only 1 hunt choice** for elk.
- For hunts with **4 or more tags**: **75% preference point draw, 25% random draw**.
- For hunts with **2-3 tags**: 1 tag goes to the preference point draw, remainder to random.
- For hunts with **1 tag**: awarded entirely by random draw.
- Only **362 elk tags** were available statewide for the 2025 season across 26 hunt zones covering Tule elk, Roosevelt elk, and Rocky Mountain elk.
- Party applications are **NOT allowed** for elk (nor for pronghorn or bighorn sheep).

### Weapon Types / Separate Draws

California does NOT run separate draws by weapon type. Instead, specific hunt codes designate weapon restrictions:
- **General** (any legal method)
- **Archery-only**
- **Muzzleloader/Archery**
- **Apprentice** (youth)

Each hunt code has its own tag quota within the single Big Game Drawing. You apply to specific hunt codes, each of which defines the zone, season, and weapon type.

### Premium vs. Non-Premium Deer Tags

California classifies deer tags into three categories:

| Category | Description | How Obtained |
|---|---|---|
| **Premium** | Limited-entry hunts with quotas; X zones and select others | Big Game Drawing only (preference point system) |
| **Restricted** | Zone-based tags with quotas that may sell out | First-come, first-served (OTC with quota) |
| **Unrestricted** | Zone-based tags with no quota cap (historically) | Over-the-counter, no quota |

Premium tags are filled by approximately July 1; restricted tags may sell out by August 1; unrestricted tags historically did not sell out before season (though B-zone tags have sold out in recent years).

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### Earning Points

- **Unsuccessful applicants** earn 1 preference point per species per year.
- You can **purchase a preference point without entering the draw** by using the "points-only" code during the application window (April 15 -- June 2).
  - Deer points-only code: **PD**
  - Elk points-only code: **499**
- A valid **Annual Hunting License** is required even for points-only purchases.

### Point Expiration

- Preference points **expire** if you do not apply (or purchase a point) for **5 consecutive years** for that species.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

- As of 2025, the maximum preference points any applicant holds is **23 points** (this applies across species; the system has been running since approximately 2002).

### Losing Points

- **Deer**: You lose ALL accumulated preference points when drawn for a premium tag as your **first choice**. Drawing on second or third choice does NOT consume points.
- **Elk/Pronghorn/Sheep**: You lose ALL accumulated preference points for the species when drawn AND you pay for the tag.

### Tag Turn-Back / Refunds

- **State law does not allow refunds** of deer tag fees once you obtain a big game permit.
- **Premium deer permits may NOT be exchanged.**
- **Non-premium deer permits** may be exchanged if certain criteria are met (see the annual Big Game Hunting Digest for specifics).
- Historically, CDFW has offered **tag return with preference point reinstatement** under special circumstances (e.g., wildfire closures). When offered:
  - Only tags acquired through the Big Game Drawing as a first choice are eligible.
  - The returned tag must be postmarked at least one day before the season starts.
  - A written statement explaining the reason for the return is required.
  - This is NOT a standard annual program; it has been offered on a case-by-case basis (e.g., 2021 fire seasons).
- For elk, pronghorn, and sheep: successful applicants must submit payment by **July 15** or the permit goes to an alternate. Failure to pay effectively returns the tag.

## 3. RESIDENT VS. NONRESIDENT RULES

### Residency Requirements

- A California resident is defined as someone who has **resided in the state continuously for at least 6 months** prior to applying for a hunting license.
- Active-duty military members stationed in California and Job Corps enrollees are considered residents.

### Deer -- No Nonresident Quota

- There is **NO separate resident/nonresident quota** for deer.
- Nonresidents compete in the **same draw pool** as residents with equal odds.
- Nonresidents may apply as part of a party (up to 6 people) for deer.

### Elk -- Severe Nonresident Limitation

- **Only 1 (one) elk tag per year** is available to nonresidents statewide across all elk hunts combined.
- Nonresidents may NOT apply as a party for elk.
- The single nonresident elk tag is awarded based on the same preference point / random draw structure.

### Pronghorn Antelope

- **Only 1 (one) antelope tag per year** to nonresidents (combined with elk: "up to one antelope and one elk permit total").
- Nonresidents may NOT apply as a party.

### Bighorn Sheep

- **Up to 10% of total bighorn sheep tags** may be issued to nonresidents (not guaranteed).

### No Waiting Period

- There is no mandatory waiting period between drawing tags in California (except for bighorn sheep, which is once-in-a-lifetime).

  - Elk: $8.13 application fee (nonrefundable).
- A valid Annual Hunting License is required in all cases.

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### OTC Deer Tags -- Yes, Available

California offers OTC (non-draw) deer tags for most general-season zones. These are classified as "restricted" or "unrestricted" tags:

| Zone Type | Examples | Tag Type | Quota? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A Zones** | A-North, A-South | Restricted (quota) | Yes, may sell out |
| **B Zones** | B-1 through B-6 | Restricted (quota) | Yes, have sold out (B zones sold out pre-season in 2016-2017) |
| **C Zones** | C-1 through C-4 | Restricted (quota) | Yes |
| **D Zones** | D-3 through D-19 | Restricted (quota) | Yes |
| **X Zones** | X-1 through X-12 | **Premium (draw only)** | Draw only |
| **Archery-Only Tag** | Valid in A, B, D zones and G-10 | Unrestricted | No quota historically |

- **A, B, C, and D zone tags** are generally available OTC (first-come, first-served) but many now have quotas and can sell out.
- **X zone tags** are premium draw-only tags -- NOT available OTC.
- The **statewide Archery-Only deer tag** is valid during archery and general seasons in A, B, and D zones and Hunt G-10 (archery methods only). It is NOT valid in C or X zones. Historically no quota.

### OTC Deer Tag Prices

Same as the tag fees listed above:
- Resident first deer tag: $41.30
- Nonresident first deer tag: $368.20
- Resident second deer tag: $51.58
- Nonresident second deer tag: $368.20

### OTC Elk Tags -- NO

- There are **NO over-the-counter elk tags** in California. All elk tags are through the Big Game Drawing (or the SHARE program, which is also a draw).

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

### Private Lands Management (PLM) Program

California's landowner tag program is the **Private Lands Management (PLM) program**, administered by CDFW.

### Program Structure

- **120 participating properties** across 26 counties, encompassing over **1.28 million acres**.
- Smallest property: 375 acres; largest: 270,000 acres; average: ~9,936 acres.
- Landowners submit a wildlife management plan and enter into a **5-year licensing agreement** with CDFW, with annual reviews.
- Biologists consult with landowners on habitat improvements (water sources, native plantings, brush piles, etc.).

### Tags and Hunting

- PLM properties receive tags for **deer, elk, pronghorn antelope, wild turkey, quail, waterfowl**, and other species.
- PLM properties often offer **expanded hunting season dates and/or increased bag limits** compared to public hunts.
- PLM tags are **property-specific**: they can only be used on the PLM property for which they are issued.
- Landowners may **transfer PLM tags** to hunters of their choosing (i.e., the landowner selects who gets to hunt).
- Hunters must surrender an **unfilled deer tag or PLM voucher** valid for the current license year in exchange for receiving a PLM deer tag.
- Many PLM properties market their hunting access commercially through outfitters.

### Volume Relative to Public Draw

- Exact numbers of PLM tags issued annually are **NOT FOUND -- needs manual verification**. CDFW does not publicly publish aggregate PLM tag totals in an easily accessible format.
- PLM tag fees are set separately by CDFW ("2026 PLM Tag Fees" are referenced but specific amounts are not published on the main PLM webpage).

### SHARE Program (Additional Opportunity)

- The **Shared Habitat Alliance for Recreational Enhancement (SHARE)** program offers additional big-game hunting opportunities on participating private and public lands, separate from the Big Game Drawing.
- Example: In spring 2026, CDFW offered 5 bull elk tags through SHARE in Del Norte County (application deadline March 11, 2026; results March 13, 2026).
