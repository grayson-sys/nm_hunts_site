# Arizona Big Game Draw System -- Deer and Elk

Last updated: 2026-03-04

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### System Type: Bonus Point Weighted Lottery (Hybrid)

Arizona uses a **bonus point system** -- not a pure preference point or straight lottery. It is a hybrid that combines a max-bonus-point pass with a weighted random lottery. Every applicant has a chance to draw, but accumulated bonus points increase odds.

### Three-Pass Draw Process

The draw is conducted in three sequential passes:

**Pass 1 -- Bonus Point Pass (20% of tags):**
- 20% of authorized hunt permit-tags in each hunt number for elk, deer, antelope, turkey, javelina, and spring bear are allocated in this pass.
- Applications with the **maximum bonus points** for that hunt number are drawn first.
- If the 20% allocation is not filled by max-point holders, the system works downward through descending point levels until the 20% is exhausted or all bonus-point applications are processed.
- Nonresidents are limited to **5% of the tags** in this pass (out of the overall 10% nonresident cap).

**Pass 2 -- Random Draw, 1st and 2nd Choices (remaining 80% of tags):**
- All unsuccessful applicants from Pass 1 receive new random numbers.
- The system processes applications in random-number order, checking 1st and 2nd hunt choices for available permits.
- Bonus points still matter here (see weighting formula below).
- Nonresidents can receive up to **5% of tags** in this pass.

**Pass 3 -- Random Draw, 3rd through 5th Choices:**
- Remaining unsuccessful applicants are re-randomized.
- The system checks 3rd, 4th, and 5th hunt selections for available permits.

### Bonus Point Weighting Math

In Passes 2 and 3, bonus points increase draw odds through a **squared weighting** system:

- The system generates **one random number for the application plus one additional random number for each bonus point** held.
- The **lowest** random number among all generated numbers becomes the application's draw number.
- Effectively, an applicant with N bonus points gets (N + 1) random numbers. More numbers = better chance of getting a low (favorable) draw number.
- This is mathematically equivalent to (bonus points + 1) chances in the draw, though the advantage compounds -- someone with 10 points has 11x the chance of someone with 0 points.

**Group applications:** For groups of 2-4 applicants, bonus points are averaged across all members and rounded to the nearest whole number (0.5+ rounds up).

### Choices Per Application

Each applicant may list **up to 5 hunt choices** on their application. Choices 1-2 are evaluated in Pass 2; choices 3-5 are evaluated in Pass 3.

### Separate Draws by Weapon Type?

Arizona does **not** run separate draws by weapon type. Rifle, archery, and muzzleloader hunts each have their own **hunt numbers** within the same draw. Applicants select specific hunt numbers (which encode unit, season, and weapon type) as their choices. All weapon types compete in the same three-pass process.

### If You Don't Draw Any Choices

- You are awarded **one additional bonus point** for that species.
- Your application fee ($13 resident / $15 nonresident) is **not refunded**.
- The tag fee is **not charged** (only charged if drawn).
- Your hunting license fee is **not refunded**.

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### PointGuard (Tag Turn-Back with Point Restoration)

Arizona does **not** allow hunters to simply "turn in" a tag and keep their points by default. However, they offer the **PointGuard** and **PointGuard Plus** programs that must be purchased at application time.

#### PointGuard (Standard)
- **Cost:** $10 per species, per applicant
- **Coverage:** One hunt permit-tag surrender per species, for one draw cycle
- **Must be purchased** during online application or before the application deadline
- **Non-refundable** fee

#### PointGuard Plus (Enhanced)
- **Cost:** $25 per applicant
- **Coverage:** Surrender a hunt permit-tag for **all eligible big game species** for **three consecutive draw cycles** from purchase date
- **Additional benefit:** Priority positioning for reissued hunt permit-tags that others have surrendered
- **Non-refundable** fee

#### Tag Surrender Rules and Deadline
- Original tag and completed Hunt Permit-Tag Surrender Form must be received at an AZGFD office **no later than 5:00 PM the day before the hunt begins**.
- Can be submitted in person at any AZGFD office or by mail to: AZGFD, Attn: Draw, 5000 W. Carefree Highway, Phoenix, AZ 85086.
- Only **one tag per species** may be surrendered.
- **Tag fee is non-refundable** regardless of surrender.

#### Effect on Points
- The department restores **all accumulated bonus points**, including the bonus point the applicant would have accrued if they had been unsuccessful in the draw.
- Without PointGuard, drawing a tag **purges all bonus points** for that species, even if you surrender the tag.

### Buying a Point Without Applying for a Hunt

**Yes.** Arizona allows "Bonus Point Only" applications. Instead of listing hunt numbers, applicants select the "Bonus Point Only" code for the species. The cost is the same as a regular application:
- Resident: $13 per species
- Nonresident: $15 per species
- A valid hunting license is required.
- You cannot list any hunt numbers alongside a Bonus Point Only application.

### Loyalty Bonus Point

After **5 consecutive years** of submitting valid applications (or bonus-point-only purchases) for a species, you earn one **loyalty bonus point**. This loyalty point is retained as long as you continue applying at least once per year for that species.

### Hunter Education Bonus Point

Successful completion of Hunter Education earns **one permanent bonus point** per species. This is a one-time, permanent addition.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

AZGFD does not prominently publish maximum point levels. Maximum point levels vary by hunt demand -- a late cow elk hunt might have a max of ~7 points, while early rifle bull elk may have applicants with 23+ points. The actual maximum fluctuates annually.

## 3. RESIDENT VS NONRESIDENT RULES

### Tag Allocation

- **Nonresidents may receive up to 10%** of the total number of tags for elk, deer, antelope, and sheep.
- This 10% is split across the draw passes:
  - Up to **5% in the Bonus Point Pass** (Pass 1)
  - Up to **5% in the Random Draw** (Passes 2 and 3)
- The 10% is a **cap, not a guarantee** -- if nonresident demand is low, fewer than 10% may be issued.
- Residents receive the remaining **90%+** of tags.

### Separate Draws or Combined?

Residents and nonresidents participate in the **same draw** with the same three-pass process. The nonresident cap is applied as a quota within the draw -- once the nonresident allocation is filled for a hunt number, remaining nonresident applications are passed over.

### Waiting Periods Between Tags

There is **no mandatory waiting period** between drawing tags for nonresidents in Arizona (unlike some states). You can apply and draw every year.

### Nonresident Caps

The 10% nonresident cap applies **statewide per hunt number**, not per unit in aggregate. Each individual hunt number has its own 10% nonresident allocation.

### Residency Requirements

To qualify as an Arizona **resident** for hunting purposes:
- Must be **domiciled** in Arizona (claims AZ as true, fixed, permanent home and principal residence)
- Must have lived in Arizona for **6 consecutive months immediately preceding** the date of application
- Must **not claim residency** in any other state or jurisdiction for any purpose
- **Active-duty military** stationed in Arizona (permanent or temporary) qualify as residents
- Military members stationed elsewhere who list Arizona as their home of record also qualify

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### OTC Archery Deer

- **Available to residents:** Yes, unlimited.
- **Available to nonresidents:** Yes, but **capped at 2,785 tags** for 2026 (first-come, first-served).
- **Weapon:** Archery only.
- **Units:** Most units are open, but some units are closed or restricted. Hunters must check the OTC Archery Deer Informational Handout for closed units.
- **Harvest limits** have been set by unit and by species; hunting in a closed unit is strictly enforced.
- **Bag limit:** One deer per calendar year. If you harvest a deer during OTC archery, you may not take another deer (archery or draw) that calendar year.
- **Nonresident tag sale:** Online only at [otcarchery.azgfd.com](https://otcarchery.azgfd.com/), first-come first-served. For 2026, tags went on sale November 3, 2025 at 8:00 AM Arizona time.
- **Season dates (typical):** Third week of August through second week of September, and mid-December through January 31.
- **Price:** $45 resident / $300 nonresident.
- **Harvest reporting:** Required within 48 hours of harvest, online or by phone.

### OTC Elk (Limited Opportunity)

- **Available to residents:** Yes.
- **Available to nonresidents:** Yes (subject to availability).
- **Weapon:** Any legal weapon (not limited to archery).
- **Units:** Limited to portions of approximately **14 units** where elk conflict with mule deer habitat or ranching operations. Elk populations in these areas are expected to be very low (as few as a dozen animals in some areas).
- **Quota:** NOT FOUND - needs manual verification (likely unlimited by tag count but limited by the small number of elk present).
- **Price:** $135 resident / $650 nonresident.
- **Hunter success:** Expected to be very low due to sparse elk populations in designated areas.
- **Season details:** Consult the annual Elk OTC Nonpermit-Tag Informational Handout from AZGFD.

### Other OTC Species

Also available OTC: mountain lion, bear, archery-only javelina, and juniors-only turkey (shotgun).

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

### Arizona Does NOT Have a Landowner Tag Program

Arizona has **no landowner tag system**. There are no tags tied to property ownership, no transferable/saleable landowner permits, and no landowner preference in the draw.

The draw is the **only way** to obtain a big game permit-tag for deer and elk (aside from OTC nonpermit-tags and leftover tags). There are also **raffle tags** for each big game species, typically sold through conservation organizations (e.g., Arizona Elk Society, Mule Deer Foundation), but these are not landowner tags.

### Landowner Relations Programs (Not Tag Programs)

AZGFD does operate landowner support programs, but none involve hunting tags:

- **Landowner Respect Program:** Provides signs, gates, fence ladders, and kiosks to manage public access on ranch lands.
- **Adopt-a-Ranch Program:** Volunteer labor for ranch maintenance projects.
- **Habitat Improvement Partnerships:** Collaborative conservation projects on private land.

- All official AZGFD data is in **PDF format only**. There is no CSV, Excel, or API access.
- PDFs are hosted on predictable S3 URLs which could potentially be scraped, but there is no official bulk download or machine-readable data feed.
- Third-party services (goHUNT, HuntScore, etc.) appear to manually digitize AZGFD PDF data annually.
- For any programmatic analysis, PDFs would need to be downloaded and parsed (e.g., with tabula or pdfplumber in Python).
