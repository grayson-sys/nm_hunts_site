# Utah Big Game Draw System -- Deer & Elk

*Last updated: March 2026*
*Researched from Utah DWR official sources and third-party hunting resources*

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### System Type: Hybrid (Bonus Points + Preference Points)

Utah uses **two distinct point systems** depending on hunt type:

- **Bonus Points** -- Used for **limited-entry** and **once-in-a-lifetime** hunts (limited-entry buck deer, bull elk, buck pronghorn, moose, bison, bighorn sheep, mountain goat). This is a **weighted lottery** (not pure preference).
- **Preference Points** -- Used for **general-season** hunts (general buck deer, antlerless deer, antlerless elk, doe pronghorn). This is a **pure preference** system (highest points draw first, no randomness).

### Bonus Point Math (Weighted Lottery)

The bonus point system uses a **squared-weight formula**:

```
Draw entries = (Bonus Points)^2 + 1
```

Examples:
| Bonus Points | Draw Entries |
|-------------|-------------|
| 0           | 1           |
| 1           | 2           |
| 3           | 10          |
| 5           | 26          |
| 10          | 101         |
| 15          | 226         |
| 20          | 401         |

### 50/50 Split for Limited-Entry Permits

For each limited-entry hunt:
- **50% of permits** go to applicants with the **maximum (highest) bonus points** for that hunt
- **50% of permits** are drawn in a **random weighted lottery** from all remaining applicants (weighted by points-squared formula above)

If there is an odd number of permits, the majority goes to the max-point-holder pool (e.g., 5 permits = 3 to max point holders, 2 to random).

### Preference Point System (General Season)

For general-season hunts, preference points work like a place in line. Applicants with the most preference points draw first, then the next tier, and so on. **No randomness** -- it is purely first-in, first-out.

### Separate Draws by Weapon Type?

Yes. Utah runs separate hunts (and therefore separate draws) for:
- **Archery**
- **Muzzleloader**
- **Any Legal Weapon (rifle)**
- **Multi-season** (covers multiple weapon seasons -- higher permit cost)

Each weapon type has its own permit quotas per unit. However, bonus/preference points are **species-specific**, not weapon-specific. Your elk bonus points apply whether you apply for archery elk or rifle elk.

### Number of Choices Per Application

Applicants may list **up to 5 ranked hunt choices** per species application. The draw processes all first-choice applicants first, then second choices, etc.

### Draw Processing Order

The draw processes species in this order:
1. Limited-entry buck deer
2. Limited-entry bull elk
3. Limited-entry buck pronghorn
4. Once-in-a-lifetime species (moose, bison, bighorn sheep, mountain goat)
5. General-season categories (buck deer, antlerless)

### What Happens If You Don't Draw Any Choice?

- You receive **one bonus point** (for limited-entry) or **one preference point** (for general-season) for that species
- Points accumulate year over year
- You may still purchase OTC elk tags (spike, any-bull, archery elk) after the draw
- Leftover/remaining permits become available after the draw on a first-come, first-served basis

### Restriction: One Limited-Entry Tag Per Year

You may not hold more than one limited-entry permit in a single calendar year. If you draw a limited-entry deer tag, you cannot also hold a limited-entry elk tag that year. However, you CAN hold a general-season tag alongside a limited-entry tag for a different species, with restrictions (see below).

**Key rule:** If you draw a limited-entry permit (elk, pronghorn, or deer), you cannot also obtain a general-season buck deer permit that same year.

### Resident vs. Nonresident Application Differences

- **Residents** may apply for **ONE** limited-entry species (buck deer OR bull elk OR buck pronghorn) plus ONE once-in-a-lifetime species per year
- **Nonresidents** may apply for **ALL THREE** limited-entry species AND all once-in-a-lifetime species in the same year (but can still only draw one)

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### Can Hunters Turn In a Tag After Drawing?

Yes. Utah allows permit surrender under specific rules.

### Surrender Rules and Deadlines

**30+ days before season opens:**
- All **previously accrued** bonus/preference points for that species are **restored**
- You do **NOT** earn a point for the current year
- Limited-entry and once-in-a-lifetime permits: refund of permit cost minus **$25 handling fee**
- General-season permits: **no refund**

**Less than 30 days before season opens:**
- You **lose ALL** previously acquired bonus/preference points for that species
- You do **NOT** earn a point for the current year
- No refund

**Medical exceptions:** Must provide physician verification on letterhead. Required forms and paperwork must be submitted within 90 days of season end for a full refund.

**Group applications:** ALL group members must surrender 30+ days before the hunt for ANY member to retain points. If one member surrenders late, all members lose points.

Source: [Utah DWR Surrender Permits & Refunds](https://wildlife.utah.gov/surrendered-permits-refunds.html)

### Can You Buy a Point Without Applying for a Hunt?

**Yes.** You can purchase a bonus point or preference point during:
1. The **spring big game application period** (March-April) -- select "bonus point only" or "preference point only" instead of applying for a hunt
2. The **June antlerless application period** -- a second chance to purchase a point if you missed the spring window

You must hold a valid Utah hunting or combination license to purchase points.

**Cost:** The application fee ($10 resident / $21 nonresident as of Sept 1, 2025) is charged for point-only purchases.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

The Utah DWR publishes points reports annually as PDFs at [wildlife.utah.gov/bg-odds.html](https://wildlife.utah.gov/bg-odds.html). The exact maximum point levels are not published as a single summary number but can be derived from the draw odds reports. For the most competitive once-in-a-lifetime species (e.g., desert bighorn sheep, moose), max point holders may have 20+ points. For limited-entry elk on premium units, max points can exceed 15-20+. Consult the annual points report for exact current maximums.

## 3. RESIDENT VS. NONRESIDENT RULES

### Tag Allocation

- **Nonresidents receive up to 10%** of permits for each individual hunt
- **Residents receive 90%+** of permits
- Low-quota hunts may yield zero nonresident tags (e.g., a hunt with only 5 tags may allocate 0 to nonresidents)

### Separate Draws or Combined?

**Separate pools.** Residents and nonresidents are placed into **their own drawing pools** and do not compete against each other. The 10% nonresident quota is drawn separately within the nonresident pool, still following the 50/50 bonus-point/random split.

### Waiting Periods Between Tags

After drawing a limited-entry permit:
- **Buck deer:** 5-year waiting period before reapplying
- **Bull elk:** 5-year waiting period before reapplying
- **Buck pronghorn:** 2-year waiting period before reapplying
- **Once-in-a-lifetime species:** Lifetime -- you cannot apply again for that species

During the waiting period, you **cannot purchase bonus points** for that species.

These waiting periods apply to **both residents and nonresidents**.

### Nonresident Caps

The 10% cap applies **per individual hunt** (unit + weapon + season combination), not statewide. There is no overall statewide cap on the total number of nonresident tags issued across all hunts.

General-season elk OTC permits do **not** have a nonresident/resident quota split.

### Residency Requirements

You are a **Utah resident** if:
- You have had a **fixed permanent home** and principal establishment in Utah for **6 consecutive months** immediately preceding the license purchase
- You do **not** claim residency for hunting/fishing/trapping in any other state or country

**Exceptions:**
- Military, religious, or educational absence from Utah preserves residency if you don't claim residency elsewhere
- Full-time students at Utah institutions: **60 consecutive days** of presence in Utah qualifies as resident (if not claiming residency elsewhere)
- Absentee landowners paying property tax do **NOT** qualify as residents

Source: [Utah DWR Residency](https://wildlife.utah.gov/utah-resident.html)

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### OTC Deer

**General-season buck deer is primarily a DRAW**, not OTC. However:
- **Leftover/remaining permits** are sold OTC after the draw on a first-come, first-served basis
- **Nonresident archery deer** OTC tags have been available (e.g., in Nov 2025, 2,785 nonresident archery deer tags sold out in 3 minutes)
- **WARNING:** If you purchase an OTC general-season deer permit after the draw, you **lose all previously accrued preference points**

### OTC Elk

Utah has significant OTC elk opportunities. These are sold starting in July, first-come, first-served:

| OTC Elk Hunt | Quota | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General any-bull elk (early season) | ~15,000 permits | Any legal weapon + muzzleloader |
| General any-bull elk (late season) | No permit cap | Unlimited |
| Spike bull elk | ~15,000 (up to 4,500 multi-season) | Specific units |
| General archery elk | Available OTC | Sold starting early July |

**Both residents and nonresidents** can purchase OTC elk permits. There is **no resident/nonresident quota** for general-season OTC elk.

### OTC Sale Dates (Typical)

- Early July: Archery elk OTC sales open
- Mid-July: Any-bull elk and spike elk OTC sales open
- Check [wildlife.utah.gov/remaining-permits.html](https://wildlife.utah.gov/remaining-permits.html) for current availability

### OTC Tag Prices

OTC elk prices are the same as the general-season elk permit prices listed in Section 4 above (e.g., $56 resident / $849 nonresident for general bull elk).

### Which Units Are OTC vs. Draw?

- **General any-bull elk units** and **spike bull elk units** are OTC
- **Limited-entry elk units** are draw-only
- **All buck deer** is draw (general season uses preference points; limited-entry uses bonus points)
- **Antlerless deer and elk** are draw

Source: [GOHUNT Utah OTC Info](https://www.gohunt.com/browse/tips-and-tricks/otc-and-leftover-tags/information-on-the-utah-tag-sale-for-general-any-bull-elk-units)

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

Utah has **two main landowner programs**: CWMU and LOA.

### CWMU (Cooperative Wildlife Management Units)

**Overview:** The CWMU program has opened more than 2 million acres of private land to public hunting. Landowners receive tags in exchange for providing public hunting access.

**Requirements:**
- Minimum **5,000 contiguous acres** for deer, pronghorn, or turkey
- Minimum **10,000 contiguous acres** for elk or moose
- Multiple landowners may combine properties to form a single CWMU

**Species covered:** Deer (buck and antlerless), elk (bull and antlerless), moose, pronghorn

**Tag Split:**
- Tags are split between the CWMU operator (private) and the public draw
- Common splits: **90/10**, **80/20**, or **70/30** (higher number to CWMU operator)
- The exact split is negotiated between the CWMU and DWR biologists
- Public hunters must receive **comparable hunting opportunity** to private hunters

**Public Access:**
- Utah residents can obtain CWMU public-draw permits through the state drawing
- Nonresidents **cannot** participate in the CWMU public drawing but **may** acquire permit vouchers directly from CWMU operators

**Transferability:** CWMU operators can sell/transfer their private-allocation vouchers to any licensed hunter, including nonresidents. Prices are set by the operator (market rate) and are typically much higher than state permit fees.

Source: [Utah DWR CWMU](https://wildlife.utah.gov/cwmu.html)

### LOA (Landowner Association)

**Overview:** The LOA program provides transferable vouchers to landowners whose private lands are within limited-entry hunting units and provide wildlife habitat.

**Tag Allocation:**
- The association receives a **percentage** of total limited-entry permits for the unit based on the amount of private land enrolled
- Typical split: LOA retains **80%** of vouchers (valid on enrolled private land only); **20%** go to public hunters with access to LOA private lands
- For general-season landowner permits: landowner must have **640 acres** of habitat; up to **6%** above total unit permits can be issued OTC

**Transferability:** LOA vouchers are **fully transferable** -- the landowner can sell the voucher to any person who holds (or obtains) a valid Utah hunting or combination license, including nonresidents.

**Price:** Set by the landowner/association (market rate). NOT the same as state draw tag prices -- typically significantly more expensive.

**Volume:** CWMU and LOA tags combined represent a meaningful but minority share of total permits on units where they exist. The exact numbers vary by unit and year. NOT FOUND - exact statewide volume data needs manual verification from annual guidebooks.

Sources:
- [Utah Admin Rule R657-43: Landowner Permits](https://wildlife.utah.gov/r657-43.html)
- [Utah Farm Bureau CWMU/LOA Changes](https://www.utahfarmbureau.org/Article/Division-of-Wildlife-Approves-Changes-to-CWMU-and-LOA-permits-supported-by-Utah-Farm-Bureau)
