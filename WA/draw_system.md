# Washington Big Game Draw System -- Deer and Elk

*Last updated: March 2026*
*Regulations reference: 2024-2026 season cycle (WAC 220-415)*

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### System Type: Squared Bonus Point Weighted Lottery

Washington uses a **weighted-point permit drawing system** (bonus point system) for special hunt permits. It is NOT a preference point system -- no permits are set aside or guaranteed for maximum-point holders. Every applicant has a chance to draw regardless of points, but higher-point applicants have better odds.

### How the Weighted Lottery Math Works

- Each year an applicant applies and is unsuccessful, they accrue **1 bonus point** in that species/category.
- At draw time, the applicant's bonus points are **squared** to determine the number of random numbers they receive in the lottery.
  - 0 points = 1 chance (first-time applicants still get 1 chance)
  - 1 point = 1 chance (1 squared)
  - 2 points = 4 chances
  - 3 points = 9 chances
  - 5 points = 25 chances
  - 10 points = 100 chances
  - 20 points = 400 chances
- The computer assigns random numbers to each chance. The applicant's lowest random number is used to rank them against other applicants. Lower number = higher priority.
- This is a pure bonus point system: **no permits are reserved for top-point holders**. A zero-point applicant can theoretically beat a 20-point applicant.

### Separate Draws by Weapon Type?

**No.** Washington does not run separate draws by weapon type for deer and elk special permits. However, hunters must select a weapon type (modern firearm, archery, or muzzleloader) when purchasing their transport tag, and specific special permit hunts are designated for particular weapon types. Points are tracked by **species category**, not by weapon type.

### Special Permit Categories

Points are tracked independently per species AND per category. The deer and elk categories include:

**Deer categories:**
- Quality Deer (limited to 2 hunt choices per application)
- Buck Deer
- Antlerless Deer
- Youth Deer
- Senior (65+) Deer
- Hunters with Disabilities Deer
- Second Deer Tag

**Elk categories:**
- Quality Elk (limited to 2 hunt choices per application)
- Bull Elk
- Antlerless Elk
- Youth Elk
- Hunters with Disabilities Elk
- Incentive Elk

### Number of Choices per Application

- **Up to 4 hunt choices** per application for most categories.
- **Up to 2 hunt choices** for Quality Deer and Quality Elk categories.
- Choices are considered in priority order. All of an applicant's choices are evaluated before moving on to the next applicant.

### Group Applications

- Maximum group size: **8 hunters** for deer and elk.
- Bonus points of all group members are **averaged** and applied to the group application.

### What Happens If You Don't Draw Any Choices?

- You are not selected for any permit.
- You **gain 1 bonus point** in that category for the unsuccessful application year.
- Your point total carries forward to the next year.

### General Season vs. Special Permit Hunts

Washington operates a **dual system**:

1. **General season hunts**: Available to anyone who purchases the appropriate big game license and transport tag (over the counter). No draw required. Available for both deer and elk statewide in designated GMUs.

2. **Special permit hunts**: Require winning a draw. Offer access to limited-entry units, antler-class restrictions (e.g., mature bucks/bulls), antlerless opportunities in controlled areas, or extended seasons. Special permits are in addition to general season -- a hunter can participate in both.

Source: [WDFW Special Hunt Permits](https://wdfw.wa.gov/hunting/special-hunts), [eRegulations Application Instructions](https://www.eregulations.com/washington/hunting/special-permit-application-instructions)

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### Can Hunters Turn In a Tag After Drawing?

**Yes.** Washington allows successful applicants for all big game special permits to return their permit to WDFW for any reason and have their **points restored**.

### Deadline

Permits must be returned **at least two weeks before the opening day of the season** for that specific hunt. Contact Wildlife Program at (360) 902-2515 to initiate the return.

### Effect on Points

- If the permit is returned before the deadline, all accumulated bonus points in that category are **restored** (the points that were zeroed upon drawing are given back).
- If the hunter keeps the permit (whether they hunt or not), points remain at zero in that category.

### Can You Buy a Point Without Applying to the Draw?

**Yes, effectively.** Washington offers a "**Point Saver**" option. Hunters can purchase a special hunt permit application and select the "Point Saver" option, which allows them to accrue 1 bonus point in that category **without being entered into the draw**. The cost is the same as a regular application:

- Resident: $9.61 per application
- Nonresident: $152.30 per application

There is no separate, cheaper "points-only" purchase -- you pay the full application fee regardless.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

Washington has **no maximum point cap**. Points have been accumulating since the bonus point system was implemented. The system has been running since the early 2000s, so hunters with 20+ years of continuous applications could hold 20+ points in some categories. Exact maximum point levels per category are not published by WDFW in a standard report.

**Note:** Because no permits are set aside for max-point holders, even 20+ points does not guarantee a draw.

Source: [eRegulations Application Instructions](https://www.eregulations.com/washington/hunting/special-permit-application-instructions), [Huntin' Fool Washington](https://www.huntinfool.com/states/washington)

## 3. RESIDENT VS. NONRESIDENT RULES

### Tag Allocation

**There is NO nonresident quota or cap for any species in Washington.** Residents and nonresidents compete in the **same draw pool** with equal odds. This is unusual among western states.

### Separate Draws?

**No.** All applicants (resident and nonresident) are combined into a single draw per category/hunt.

### Waiting Periods

None. Nonresidents may apply every year with no waiting period.

### Nonresident Caps

**None.** No percentage-based allocation, no numerical cap.

### Residency Requirements

To qualify as a Washington **resident** for hunting license purposes:
- Must maintain a **permanent place of abode** within Washington for at least **90 days** preceding license purchase.
- Must establish intent to continue residing in Washington through formal evidence (driver's license, voter registration, etc.).
- Cannot hold a resident hunting or fishing license in another state.
- **Military exception:** Active-duty military with orders showing Washington as duty station qualify as residents.

Source: [WDFW Hunting Licenses](https://wdfw.wa.gov/licenses/hunting), [OnX Hunt Washington](https://www.onxmaps.com/hunt/blog/hunting-application-details-washington)

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### OTC Deer: YES

General season deer tags are available **over the counter** (no draw required) to anyone who purchases the appropriate big game license. Washington has three deer species:

- **Blacktail deer** (Western Washington)
- **White-tailed deer** (Eastern Washington)
- **Mule deer** (Eastern Washington)

All have general season OTC opportunities in most GMUs. Antler restrictions vary by GMU (e.g., any buck, 3-point minimum).

### OTC Elk: YES

General season elk tags are available **over the counter**. Washington has two elk subspecies:

- **Roosevelt elk** (Western Washington) -- 3-point minimum during general rifle/archery seasons.
- **Rocky Mountain elk** (Eastern Washington) -- **spike only** during general rifle and archery seasons.

Hunters must choose either Eastern or Western Washington and purchase the corresponding transport tag.

### Which Units Are General vs. Special Permit?

Most GMUs across the state have general season opportunities for deer and elk. Special permit hunts exist in addition to (or sometimes instead of) general seasons in specific units. Some units are **special permit only** for certain sex/age classes (e.g., mature bull elk in many Eastern WA units require a special permit). The full list of GMU-level designations is published annually in the [Big Game Hunting Pamphlet](https://wdfw.wa.gov/hunting/regulations) and on [eRegulations](https://www.eregulations.com/washington/hunting/elk-general-seasons).

### Unlimited or Quota?

General season OTC tags are **unlimited** -- there is no quota on the number of general season licenses sold. Anyone who buys a license can hunt the general seasons.

### OTC Tag Prices

Same as the big game license prices listed in Section 4 above. The license IS the tag for general season purposes.

Source: [eRegulations Elk General Seasons](https://www.eregulations.com/washington/hunting/elk-general-seasons), [eRegulations Deer General Seasons](https://www.eregulations.com/washington/hunting/deer-general-seasons)

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

### Program Name: Landowner Hunting Permit (LHP) and Damage Prevention Permits

Washington does **not** have a traditional "landowner tag" system like Colorado or New Mexico. Instead, it operates two related programs:

### 1. Landowner Hunting Permit (LHP) Program

- Provides **public hunting access** to private property in exchange for specialized hunting seasons on cooperating lands.
- Landowners are allocated permits based on geographic area, population management goals, and/or damage issues.
- A landowner can only participate in **one LHP statewide**.
- Permits are **area-specific** (tied to the cooperating property).

### 2. Damage Prevention Permits

- Landowners experiencing crop damage from deer or elk may enter a **Damage Prevention Cooperative Agreement (DPCA)** with WDFW.
- Upon approval, the landowner receives damage prevention permits authorizing hunters to take specified animals on the property.
- The **landowner provides the permit to the hunter** -- permits are transferable from landowner to hunter in this sense, but they are not commercially saleable on the open market.
- Hunters must hold a valid big game license and a damage deer/elk license or tag.

### Annual Kill Quotas (Damage Prevention Permits)

- **Deer:** 300 per license year (statewide)
- **Elk:** 200 (Eastern WA), 100 (Western WA), 70 (Hanford Area), 100 spike/antlerless or 60 bulls (Elk Area 3721)
- **Turkey:** 300 per license year

### Volume Relative to Public Draw Tags

Damage prevention permits are a **small fraction** of total harvest opportunity. The 300 statewide deer damage permits and ~470 elk damage permits compare to thousands of special hunt permits issued through the public draw annually (e.g., 2,272 second deer tags alone in 2025, plus thousands more across other categories). General season harvest dwarfs both.

### Transferable/Saleable?

Permits are **not commercially saleable**. They are issued by WDFW to landowners, who then designate specific hunters. They cannot be sold on the open market like Colorado landowner vouchers.

Source: [WAC 220-440-200](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=220-440-200), [WDFW Damage Claims](https://wdfw.wa.gov/species-habitats/living/nuisance-wildlife/damage)

## SUMMARY OF KEY DISTINGUISHING FEATURES

1. **Squared bonus points** -- not preference points. No guaranteed draw for anyone.
2. **No nonresident quota** -- residents and nonresidents compete equally in the same pool.
3. **General season tags are unlimited OTC** -- no draw needed for general deer or elk.
4. **Point Saver option** -- pay full application fee to bank a point without entering the draw.
5. **Tag turn-back allowed** -- return permit 2+ weeks before season to restore points.
6. **No landowner tags for sale** -- only damage prevention permits, not commercially transferable.
7. **Draw data in Power BI** -- not easily downloadable or automatable.
8. **Harvest data in HTML tables** -- 2013-2024 available but no bulk download.
