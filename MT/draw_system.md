# Montana Big Game Draw System -- Deer & Elk

*Last updated: March 2026*

> **⚠️ IMPORTANT — DO NOT OVERSIMPLIFY:**
> Montana is NOT just "residents OTC, nonresidents draw." That's only true for
> **general season** hunting. For **limited-entry permits** (the prestigious
> trophy bull elk units like Missouri Breaks, Breaks country, and other
> controlled districts), BOTH residents AND nonresidents must enter the bonus
> point draw. Resident draw odds for these permits are just as real and just as
> relevant as nonresident odds. A Montana resident cannot walk into a sporting
> goods store and buy a Missouri Breaks bull elk tag — they accumulate bonus
> points over years and compete in the same weighted lottery. Resident LE permit
> odds must be captured and displayed in the app.

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### System Type: Hybrid (Bonus Points + Preference Points)

Montana uses **two separate point systems** that serve different purposes:

- **Preference Points** -- Used ONLY by nonresidents for drawing combination licenses (Big Game Combo, Elk Combo, Deer Combo). This is a pure preference system where 75% of combo licenses go to highest-point holders first, and 25% are awarded randomly.
- **Bonus Points** -- Used by BOTH residents and nonresidents for limited-entry permits (special permits for specific hunting districts). This is a weighted lottery where points are **squared** to determine the number of entries.

### Bonus Point Squaring Formula

Each applicant gets: **(bonus points x bonus points) + 1 = total entries**

| Bonus Points | Entries in Draw |
|-------------|----------------|
| 0           | 1              |
| 1           | 2              |
| 2           | 5              |
| 3           | 10             |
| 5           | 26             |
| 10          | 101            |
| 15          | 226            |
| 20          | 401            |
| 24 (max)    | 577            |

Every applicant always gets at least 1 entry (the "+1"), so even zero-point applicants have a chance.

### Draw Sequence (Critical for Nonresidents)

Montana's draw operates in a specific sequence:

1. **Nonresident Combination License Draw** runs FIRST. Nonresidents must draw a combo license before they can even participate in limited-entry permit draws.
2. **Limited-Entry Permit Draw** runs SECOND, only for those who hold a valid combo license (residents buy theirs OTC; nonresidents must have drawn one in step 1).

### Separate Draws by Weapon Type?

Montana does **not** have separate draws for rifle, archery, and muzzleloader in the way some states do. Instead:

- The **General Season** license (included in the combo) is valid across weapon types during their respective season dates (archery, rifle, muzzleloader).
- **Limited-entry permits** are specific to a hunting district and sometimes to a weapon type, but you apply for specific permit codes rather than entering separate weapon draws.

### Number of Choices Per Application

Applicants may list **up to 3 choices** per species for limited-entry permits.

- **First choice**: Bonus points (squared) are applied. If you draw your first choice, your bonus points for that species reset to zero.
- **Second and third choices**: Drawn randomly (no bonus points applied). Drawing a second or third choice does NOT consume your bonus points.

### What Happens If You Don't Draw Any Choices?

- You earn +1 bonus point for that species (if you opted in to the bonus point system).
- Your existing bonus points carry forward indefinitely (bonus points are never purged for non-application).
- For nonresidents who fail to draw a combo license: you earn +1 preference point (max 3), and your limited-entry application is never even processed.

### General vs. Limited Entry

- **General tags**: Included in the combination license (for nonresidents) or purchased OTC (for residents). Valid for hunting in open general-season districts statewide.
- **Limited-entry permits**: Required for specific hunting districts with controlled quotas. These are the permits that use the bonus point system.

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### Can Hunters Turn In a Tag After Drawing to Keep/Gain Points?

**No.** Montana explicitly states: "Preference points and bonus points cannot be reinstated due to refund of the licenses." If you draw a tag and then return it for a refund, you do NOT get your points back.

### Refund Deadlines and Percentages (Nonresident Combo Licenses)

| Deadline                               | Refund % |
|----------------------------------------|----------|
| Postmarked by August 1                 | 80%      |
| After August 1, before general season  | 50%      |
| Medical emergency                      | 90%      |
| Death of immediate family member       | 90%      |
| Death of licensee                      | 100%     |

- Conservation license, base hunting license, and AISPP are **non-refundable** in all cases.
- Application fees ($5 or $50) are **non-refundable**.

### Exception: SuperTag Winners

If you win a SuperTag and surrender a previously drawn special permit or license, FWP will refund the license fee AND reinstate any bonus points used for that permit.

### Can You Buy a Point Without Applying?

**Yes.** Both residents and nonresidents can purchase bonus points without applying for a permit during the points-only window (July 1 -- September 30).

- **Residents**: $15 per species (points-only purchase); $2 per species if purchased at time of application
- **Nonresidents**: $20-$25 per species for bonus points; $100 for preference points (combo license)

Nonresidents can also purchase preference points (for combo license drawing) during the window of July 1 -- December 31 for $100.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

- **Bonus points (deer/elk)**: Up to **24 points** heading into the 2026 draw (the system has been in place since approximately 2002).
- **Preference points (NR combo)**: Maximum of **3 points** (hard cap; you cannot accumulate more than 3).

## 3. RESIDENT VS. NONRESIDENT RULES

### Tag Allocation

**General Combination Licenses (Nonresident):**
- **Big Game Combo + Elk Combo**: Combined cap of approximately **17,000** licenses per year.
- **Deer Combo**: Historically ~14,500; reduced by approximately 2,500 for 2026-2027, bringing the total to approximately **12,000** nonresident deer combo licenses.
- 75% of combo licenses allocated by preference points, 25% random.

**Limited-Entry Permits:**
- Nonresidents receive up to **10%** of the permit quota per hunt code.
- If a hunting district has fewer than 10 total permits, nonresident applications may not be considered for that district.

### Separate Draws or Combined?

- **Combination license draw**: Separate pools for residents (OTC) vs. nonresidents (draw).
- **Limited-entry permit draw**: Residents and nonresidents draw from the same pool, but nonresidents are capped at 10% of permits per hunt code. The draw runs resident landowner applicants first, then nonresident landowner, then general resident, then general nonresident.

### Waiting Periods

Montana does **not** impose a mandatory waiting period between drawing limited-entry permits for deer or elk. However, for premium species (moose, sheep, goat), there is typically a once-in-a-lifetime or long waiting period restriction.

### Nonresident Caps

- Caps are applied **per hunt code** for limited-entry permits (10% cap).
- The combo license cap is applied **statewide** (17,000 combined Big Game + Elk combos; ~12,000 Deer combos for 2026).

### Residency Requirements

To qualify as a Montana resident for hunting license purposes:
- Must have **physically lived in Montana for at least 180 consecutive days** immediately prior to purchasing a resident license.
- Must NOT possess or have applied for resident hunting/fishing/trapping privileges in another state or country.
- Must file Montana state income tax returns as a resident (if required to file).
- Once established, must physically reside in Montana for not less than **120 days per year** (need not be consecutive).
- **Military exception**: Active-duty military stationed in Montana for 30+ days (plus spouse/dependents) qualify as residents.

### NR Combo License Requirements

All nonresidents must hold a combination license to hunt deer and/or elk. The combo includes:
- General deer tag and/or general elk tag (depending on combo type)
- Season fishing license
- Upland bird license

Before purchasing any combo, nonresidents must also separately purchase:
- Conservation license ($10)
- Base hunting license ($50)
- Aquatic Invasive Species Prevention Pass (AISPP) ($7.50)

### Landowner Sponsor Program (NR Deer)

Montana landowners with 640+ contiguous acres can sponsor nonresident deer hunters. FWP distributes **2,000 sponsored combination licenses** annually through this program, with up to two hunters per landowner.

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### OTC Deer -- Residents Only

- **Residents** can purchase a general deer tag **over the counter** without entering a draw. This tag is valid for the general deer season in open hunting districts statewide.
- **Nonresidents cannot** purchase OTC deer tags. They must apply for and draw a Deer Combo or Big Game Combo license.

### OTC Elk -- Residents Only

- **Residents** can purchase a general elk tag **over the counter**. This is valid for general elk season in open districts.
- **Nonresidents cannot** purchase OTC elk tags. They must draw an Elk Combo or Big Game Combo license.

### Which Units Are OTC vs. Draw?

- **General season districts**: Open to anyone holding a general tag (residents OTC, nonresidents via combo). Most of Montana's hunting districts have general season opportunities for deer and elk.
- **Limited-entry districts**: Require a special permit obtained through the bonus-point draw. These are specific hunting districts with controlled quotas, typically offering higher-quality hunting or managing specific population objectives.
- The distinction is by **hunting district**, not by broad region. FWP publishes annual regulations specifying which districts are general vs. limited entry.

### Unlimited or Quota?

- Resident general tags are effectively **unlimited** -- any resident can buy one OTC.
- Nonresident combo licenses are **quota-based** (~17,000 combined Big Game + Elk combos; ~12,000 Deer combos for 2026).

### OTC Tag Prices (Residents)

- General Deer: $16 (plus $20 in prerequisite fees = $36 total)
- General Elk: $20 (plus $20 in prerequisite fees = $40 total)

### Leftover/Surplus Tags for Nonresidents

After the draw, if combo licenses remain unissued, they may become available through:
- **Nonresident Combination License Alternate List** -- a secondary drawing for unsold combos.
- Surplus licenses are occasionally sold OTC to nonresidents, but this is not guaranteed and depends on remaining inventory.

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

### Landowner Preference Program

Montana's Landowner Preference system reserves **15%** of each hunting district's limited-entry permit quota for qualifying landowner applicants.

**Qualification requirements:**
- **Deer/Antelope**: Must own at least **160 acres** used primarily for agriculture within the target hunting district.
- **Elk permits**: Must own at least **640 contiguous acres** that is used by elk and verified by FWP within the hunting district.
- **Elk damage preference**: Requires 160+ contiguous agricultural acres with documented elk damage verified by FWP within the previous two years.

### 454 Agreements (Public Elk Hunting Access Agreements)

Named after House Bill 454 (2001), these are agreements between landowners and FWP:

- **How it works**: A landowner receives an elk license/permit in exchange for allowing hunting access to a minimum of **3 public hunters** (1 chosen by the landowner, 2 randomly selected by FWP).
- **Permit allocation**: Capped at **10%** of the hunting district's existing elk quota for 454 agreements. If demand exceeds supply, FWP holds a random drawing among qualifying landowners.
- **Designees**: The landowner may designate the license/permit benefit to an immediate family member or an authorized full-time employee.

### Transferable/Saleable?

**No.** Montana landowner permits are **not transferable or saleable** to third parties under current law. The landowner (or designated family member/employee) must be the hunter. This contrasts with states like New Mexico or Colorado where landowner tags can be transferred.

There has been ongoing debate about making 454 permits transferable, particularly through organizations like PERC, but as of 2026 they remain non-transferable.

### Nonresident Landowner Combo License Priority Pool

Nonresident landowners who own qualifying acreage in Montana can apply through a **priority pool** for combination licenses. This is a separate pathway that does not count against the general nonresident combo quota.

### Volume Relative to Public Draw Tags

- Landowner preference accounts for up to **15%** of limited-entry permits per district.
- 454 agreements add up to **10%** additional permits on top of the existing quota.
- The **Landowner Sponsor Program** distributes **2,000** nonresident deer combo licenses annually.
- The bulk of permits (75%+) go through the standard public draw.
