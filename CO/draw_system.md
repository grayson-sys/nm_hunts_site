# Colorado Big Game Draw System -- Deer & Elk

*Last updated: March 2026*
*Applies to: 2025-2026 draw cycles (major changes coming 2028)*

---

## 1. DRAW SYSTEM MECHANICS

### System Type: Pure Preference Point (Deer & Elk)

Colorado uses a **true preference point system** for deer, elk, antelope, and bear. This is NOT a weighted/bonus system for these species -- it functions like a strict queue where the applicants with the most preference points draw first. Tags are awarded to the highest point holders before anyone with fewer points.

**Note:** Moose, Rocky Mountain bighorn sheep, and mountain goat use a separate **weighted lottery** system (3 preference points max, then weighted bonus points). This document focuses on deer and elk.

### How Preference Points Work

- One preference point is earned per species each year you apply and are **unsuccessful** on your **first choice**.
- Points accumulate by **species** (not by hunt code/unit). An elk point can be used toward any elk hunt code.
- There is **no maximum cap** on preference points for deer or elk -- points accumulate indefinitely.
- If you draw your **first choice**, all preference points for that species reset to **zero**.
- If you draw your **2nd, 3rd, or 4th choice**, you **keep** your preference points and gain one additional point for the year (because you were unsuccessful on your first choice).
- You must apply or hold a license for a species **at least once every 10 consecutive years** or lose all preference points for that species.

### Number of Choices Per Application

Applicants may list up to **4 hunt code choices** per species on their application. You are not required to list all four.

### Draw Order

1. All **first-choice** applications are processed first, sorted by preference point level (highest first). Among applicants with the same number of points, a random number determines order.
2. After all first-choice applications are processed, remaining licenses are filled from **second-choice** applications (randomly, no preference points used).
3. Then third-choice, then fourth-choice -- each round is random among remaining applicants.

### Separate Draws by Weapon Type?

Rifle, archery, and muzzleloader hunts have **separate hunt codes**, but they are all processed within the **same draw** for each species. Preference points are species-wide and apply regardless of weapon type chosen.

### What Happens If You Don't Draw Any Choice?

- You receive one additional preference point for that species.
- You are refunded the license fee (application fee is non-refundable).

### 2028 System Change (Upcoming)

Starting with the **2028 season**, Colorado will switch to a **50/50 hybrid system**: half of each hunt code's quota will go through the preference point draw (highest points first) and the other half will go through a **bonus draw** (random, weighted by points). This is a major change that will significantly affect strategy for point holders.

## 2. POINT BANKING / TAG TURN-BACK

### Can You Turn In a Tag After Drawing?

**Yes.** Colorado allows license surrender with preference point restoration under these conditions:

- **Primary draw licenses:** Must be surrendered by the posted deadline (e.g., **June 1, 2026** for the 2026 primary draw). The hunter receives a refund of the license fee and preference points are restored to pre-draw levels.
- **Secondary draw licenses:** Must be surrendered by approximately **July 9** (2026).
- **General rule:** A license may be returned for a refund and point restoration no later than **30 days prior** to the start of the season (14 days for turkey).
- **After the 30-day cutoff:** Point restoration/refunds are only available for medical circumstances, military service, or extreme extenuating circumstances.

### Effect on Points of Turning Back a Tag

If surrendered within the allowed window, preference points are **restored to pre-draw level** (as if the draw never happened). No points are gained or lost.

### Reissue of Returned Tags

- Tags that required **5 or more preference points** to draw are manually reissued to the next eligible applicant.
- Tags that required **fewer than 5 points** go onto the leftover list for public purchase.

### Can You Buy a Point Without Applying for a Hunt?

**Yes.** You can submit a "preference point only" application using special hunt codes:
- Elk: `E-P-999-99-P`
- Deer: `D-P-999-99-P`

This must be done during the **primary draw application period** (by April 7, 2026). There is no separate point-purchase window, and points cannot be purchased during the secondary draw.

### Cost to Purchase a Preference Point

For deer and elk, the preference point itself is **free ($0)**. However, you must still pay:
- Application fee ($8.93 resident / $11.49 nonresident)
- Qualifying license (if you don't already have one)
- Habitat stamp (if applicable)

For moose, sheep, and mountain goat, the preference point fee is **$50 resident / $100 nonresident**.

### Maximum Points Currently Held

There is **no published maximum** cap on deer/elk preference points. CPW does not prominently publish the highest individual point totals, but some hunt codes require 20+ points to draw, indicating applicants in that range exist. The annual preference point issue of Colorado Outdoors Magazine and the Draw Recap reports show point levels needed per hunt code.

## 3. RESIDENT VS NONRESIDENT RULES

### Tag Allocation Percentages

**Deer, Elk, Antelope, Bear:**
- Hunt codes requiring **fewer than 6 resident preference points** to draw: up to **25%** of tags may go to nonresidents.
- Hunt codes requiring **6 or more resident preference points** to draw: up to **20%** of tags may go to nonresidents.
- If licenses remain after all first-choice resident applications are filled, nonresident allocation may increase.

**Sheep, Mountain Goat, Moose:**
- Nonresidents limited to **10%** of total permits.

### Separate Draws or Combined?

Residents and nonresidents are drawn from the **same pool** but with separate quotas enforced per hunt code. The draw processes resident and nonresident applications together but caps nonresident allocations at the percentages above.

### Waiting Periods Between Tags

**No mandatory waiting period** between consecutive years for deer or elk for either residents or nonresidents. You can apply and draw every year. (Some other states impose waiting periods; Colorado does not for deer/elk.)

### Nonresident Caps

Nonresident caps are applied **per hunt code**, not statewide. There is no statewide cap on total nonresident deer or elk licenses -- only the per-hunt-code percentage limits described above.

### Residency Requirements

To qualify as a Colorado resident for hunting:
- Must be **domiciled in Colorado** for at least **6 consecutive months** immediately prior to purchase.
- Must have a valid Colorado driver's license or state ID issued at least 6 months prior, **or** provide alternative documentation of 6-month residency.
- Residency is terminated if you: obtain a resident hunting/fishing license in another state, register to vote outside Colorado, obtain a driver's license showing an out-of-state address, or move out of state with intent to establish residence elsewhere.

### Group Applications

Residents and nonresidents **cannot** apply together in group applications for Rocky Mountain bighorn sheep or mountain goat. For deer and elk, group applications are allowed (mixed resident/nonresident groups are possible), but the group draws at the level of the member with the **fewest** preference points.

## 5. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) TAGS

### OTC Deer

**No OTC deer tags exist in Colorado.** All deer hunting requires a limited draw license, leftover tag, or landowner voucher. This applies to both residents and nonresidents.

### OTC Elk

OTC elk licenses are available but limited in scope:

**2nd and 3rd Rifle Season Bull Elk:**
- Available to both **residents and nonresidents**.
- Valid in many (but not all) GMUs, primarily in western Colorado.
- These are **unlimited** quantity -- not quota-based or first-come-first-served.
- Antler restrictions apply (e.g., antlered-only).
- As of 2026, GMUs 54, 55, and 551 (Gunnison Basin) have been moved from OTC to **limited draw**. GMUs 11, 12, 13, 23, 24, 131, 211, 231 moved back to unlimited/OTC status.

**Archery Elk (Nonresidents):**
- Starting in 2025, nonresident archery elk moved from OTC to **limited draw** for most of the state.
- Nonresidents can still purchase OTC archery elk in certain GMUs **east of I-25** (e.g., hunt codes E-F-133-U1-A and E-E-087-U1-A).
- Resident archery elk remains available as OTC in many units.

**OTC Cow Elk:**
- Some cow-only licenses are available OTC in select units.

### OTC Purchase Details

- OTC licenses go on sale starting **August 4, 2026** (9 a.m. MT) for the 2026 season.
- No qualifying license required for OTC purchases.
- No preference points used or affected.
- Can be purchased online at cpwshop.com or at license agents.

### OTC Elk Prices

OTC elk license prices are the same as draw elk prices:
- Resident: $67.86
- Nonresident: $825.03 (approximately $845.16 with all fees per some sources)

### Consult the Official Brochure

For a complete list of which GMUs are OTC vs. limited draw, refer to the **2026 Colorado Big Game Brochure** published by CPW. The list changes year-to-year.

## 6. LANDOWNER TAGS

Colorado has **two distinct** landowner hunting programs:

### A. Landowner Preference Program (LPP)

**Overview:** Gives qualifying private landowners preference for obtaining big game hunting licenses in their GMU.

**Qualifying Criteria:**
- Must own at least **160 contiguous acres** of agricultural land in a limited-draw hunting unit.
- Land must provide habitat for the targeted species.

**Tag Allocation:**
- Approximately **10%** of total tag quota for units **west of I-25**.
- Approximately **15%** of total tag quota for units **east of I-25**.
- Allocation is based on deeded acreage -- larger ranches receive more vouchers.

**Voucher System:**
- Vouchers are **unit-specific and species-specific**.
- They can be designated as **private-land-only** or **unit-wide**.
- Landowners can convert a voucher into a license for themselves **or transfer it to a designated hunter** (family, guests, or paying clients).
- Once transferred, **no further transfers** are allowed (single transfer only).
- **Restricted Quota Pool vouchers** (10% of general public quota) are private-land-only and may only be transferred to immediate family or youth (ages 12-17).

**Price:** Voucher holders pay the standard license fee to convert to a valid license. The voucher itself has no separate cost from CPW (though landowners may charge hunters a fee privately).

### B. Ranching for Wildlife (RFW)

**Overview:** A cooperative program between CPW and large ranches, started as a pilot in 1986. Designed to improve public hunting access on private land while giving landowners wildlife management tools and extended seasons.

**Participating Ranches:** Currently **25 ranches** enrolled, providing approximately **1 million acres** of public access.

**Qualifying Criteria:**
- Minimum approximately **10,000+ contiguous deeded acres** (varies; ranches range from ~10,960 to ~154,320 acres).
- Must demonstrate suitable wildlife habitat.
- Must commit to habitat improvement plans for game and non-game species.

**Species Covered:** Elk, deer, pronghorn, bighorn sheep, turkey, and moose.

**Tag Allocation:**
- **Male/antlered licenses:** Landowner receives **80-90%**; public receives **10-20%**.
- **Female/antlerless licenses:** **100%** allocated to public hunters.
- Exact split is negotiated between the landowner and CPW for each ranch.
- Public hunters for RFW properties are selected through a **separate limited draw** conducted by CPW.

**Transferability:** Landowners can use their allocated licenses themselves, donate them to conservation organizations, or sell guided hunts using them. Approximately one-third of RFW ranches donate hunts to conservation groups. About two-thirds provide youth hunting opportunities.

**Season Flexibility:** RFW ranches often receive **extended or custom season dates** as part of their management agreement with CPW.

### Volume Relative to Public Draw Tags

Landowner tags (LPP + RFW combined) represent a meaningful but minority share of total licenses. LPP allocations of 10-15% per GMU are drawn from the same quota as public draw tags. RFW tags are a separate allocation negotiated per ranch.

## 8. DATA SOURCES

### Draw Odds / Results

**Primary URL:** [https://cpw.state.co.us/hunting/big-game/deer/statistics](https://cpw.state.co.us/hunting/big-game/deer/statistics) (deer) and [https://cpw.state.co.us/hunting/big-game/elk/statistics](https://cpw.state.co.us/hunting/big-game/elk/statistics) (elk)

**Available Reports:**

| Report | Format | Years Available |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Draw Recap Report | PDF | 2020-2025 (latest 6 years maintained) |
| Drawn Out At Report | PDF | 2020-2025 |
| Secondary Draw Recap Report | PDF | 2021-2025 |
| Harvest Reports | PDF | 2019-2024 |
| Population Estimates | PDF | 2019-2024 |
| OTC Sales Reports | PDF | 2019-2023 |

**File Format:** All reports are published as **PDF files** hosted on CPW's content delivery system (cpw.widen.net / cpw.widencollective.com). There is **no CSV, Excel, or API** access provided by CPW.

**Draw Recap Report Contents:** Pre-draw application counts by hunt code and preference point level, plus post-draw results showing licenses awarded, point levels of successful applicants, and remaining applicants.

**Drawn Out At Report Contents:** Shows the last preference point level and choice number at which a license was drawn for each hunt code, broken out by application type (individual vs. group) and residency.

### Harvest Data

**Primary URL:** Same statistics pages as above, plus:
- [CPW Digital Collections (Historical)](https://cpw.cvlcollections.org/collections/show/9)

**Format:** PDF reports with statewide harvest estimates by species, unit, season, and method of take. Includes data on licenses issued, number of hunters, and animals harvested.

### Historical Data Limitations

- CPW currently maintains only the **most recent 6 years** of draw statistics on their website.
- In 2024, CPW removed 20+ years of historical draw data from their website, citing ADA compliance requirements.
- Older historical data may be available through the [CPW Digital Collections](https://cpw.cvlcollections.org/collections/show/9).
- Third-party services (HuntScore, GOHUNT, Huntin' Fool) maintain longer historical datasets but may require paid subscriptions.

### Can Data Be Auto-Downloaded?

- PDFs on cpw.widen.net have direct download URLs and **can be programmatically downloaded** (no login required).
- There is **no official API** for draw data.
- Data extraction from PDFs requires parsing (e.g., tabula, pdfplumber) since no structured data format (CSV/JSON) is provided.
- The CPW website itself does not require authentication to access statistics pages.
